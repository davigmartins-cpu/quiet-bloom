04 · Toy Rotation System

Less toys.
More imagination.

When children have fewer toys available, they engage more deeply and play more creatively. The rotation system makes old toys feel new — without buying anything.

"Children who have too many toys become bored and overstimulated. Simplicity is the key to sustained attention." — Kim John Payne, Simplicity Parenting

Toys out at one time
For a 3-month-old: 3 toys is the sweet spot. One to look at, one to touch, one to kick.

The 20-minute rule: If your baby has been playing happily for 20+ minutes with one simple object, that's deep focus. Don't interrupt it. That's the goal.
05 · Screen-Free Week Plan

Seven days.
Seven simple moments.

No screens, no apps, no "educational" videos. Just presence. Each day has one simple activity — nothing to buy, nothing to prepare.

"The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends zero screen time for babies under 18 months (except video calls). Not 'limited.' Zero." — AAP, 2016
0 of 7 days0%
The real purpose of screen-free time isn't the absence of screens. It's the presence of you. Boredom, silence, and unstructured time are not problems to solve — they're the conditions in which babies learn to self-regulate.
06 · Bored Is Good

Your baby cries.
You don't have to fix it.

The urge to immediately stimulate a fussing baby is deeply human — and often counterproductive. This is your guide to sitting with the discomfort so your baby can learn to sit with theirs.

"When we rush to entertain or soothe every moment of fussiness, we rob babies of the chance to develop their most important skill: self-regulation." — Dr. Becky Kennedy
02:00
Wait before intervening
Give your baby 2 minutes before picking up or stimulating. You might be surprised.
The "Pause Before Jumping In" Protocol
This is hard. The instinct to soothe immediately is love. But boredom, mild frustration, and quiet fussing are how babies build their capacity for self-regulation — one of the most important skills they'll ever develop. You're not neglecting them. You're teaching them.
07 · Say No to Gifts

Your family means well.
That's the hard part.

The people who love your baby most are often the biggest source of toy overflow. These templates help you set boundaries with kindness — and hold them.

"It's not about saying no to love. It's about saying yes to a simpler kind of it." — Kim John Payne
💬 Text / WhatsApp Message
Hi [Name] — we're so excited for you to meet [Baby]! We're keeping things really simple at home, so instead of toys or clothes, the most meaningful gifts are your time and presence. A visit, a home-cooked meal, or help around the house means more to us than anything from a store. If you'd really like to give something, a book is always welcome, or a contribution to their little savings fund. Thank you so much for understanding — and for loving our baby so well. 🤍
📧 Email — For Birthdays & Holidays
Subject: [Baby's name]'s birthday — a note from us Dear family and friends, As [Baby] turns [age], we find ourselves reflecting on what truly matters in childhood. We've been intentionally simplifying our home, and we've noticed how much calmer, more focused, and more joyful [he/she] is with fewer things. So this year, we're kindly asking for no toys or gifts. Instead, we'd love: — A favorite book from your own childhood — A handwritten letter or memory to put in their keepsake box — A day out together, just you and them — A contribution to their college savings (529 fund) Your presence in [Baby's] life is the greatest gift. Thank you for understanding and supporting the way we're choosing to raise [him/her]. With so much love, [Your names]
🗣️ Script — In-Person Conversation
When someone says: "But I just want to get them something!" You say: "That means so much — and honestly, the best thing you can give them is your time. Would you want to come over one afternoon and just be with them? That's what they'll remember." When someone says: "It's just a small toy, it won't hurt." You say: "I know you mean so well. We're trying to keep their space really simple right now — it actually helps them focus better and get overwhelmed less easily. A book would be perfect if you'd like to bring something." When someone pushes back: "I hear you, and I know this is different. We're not saying no to love — just to stuff. [Baby] is so lucky to have you in their life."
